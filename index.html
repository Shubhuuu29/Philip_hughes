About Philip Hughes

Philip Joel Hughes (30 November 1988 â€“ 27 November 2014) was an Australian cricketer who played for the national team in all formats of the game. He was known for his aggressive batting style and his resilience in the face of adversity. Hughes tragically passed away at the age of 25 after being struck on the neck by a bouncer during a domestic match.

Notable Achievements

  • Scored a century on his Test debut against South Africa in 2009.
  • Youngest Australian cricketer to score two centuries in a Test match.
  • Played in both domestic and international cricket leagues.
  • Remembered for his contribution to Australian cricket.
